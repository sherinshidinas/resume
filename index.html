Low-Cost Emergency Ventilator using Arduino, UNO

The Low-Cost Emergency Ventilator is a life-saving project developed using Arduino UNO, aimed at providing an affordable and efficient ventilator solution during emergencies, such as the COVID-19 pandemic. This ventilator utilizes Arduino UNO as its core controller to regulate air pressure and flow, helping patients with breathing difficulties to receive adequate oxygen supply. The project includes various sensors and actuators to monitor and control critical parameters like air pressure, volume, and respiratory rate. The Arduino UNO communicates with a user interface, allowing medical personnel to adjust ventilation settings and monitor patient data in real-time. The goal of this project is to offer a low-cost alternative to traditional ventilators, ensuring that medical facilities can provide life-saving support to a larger number of patients, particularly in resource-constrained environments. Please note that this project may require compliance with relevant medical regulations and standards before its practical deployment in clinical settings
